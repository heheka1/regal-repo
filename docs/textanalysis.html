Analyizing Franklin D. Roosevelt and Herbert Hoover's Speech Patterns

FDR and Hoover were presidents who served around the same time, and they both faced the challenges that came about with the Great Depression, but besides that, their legacy in American history couldn't be more different. This can be seen with the fact Hoover only served a single term as president, where as FDR would go on to serve three terms, being the only president to do so. Because of these facts, I thought it would be interesting to analyize and compare the words and phrases they use in their speeches. To do this, I used text analysis tools like AntConc and Voyant to help me pick up any patterns that I see.

FDR

Franklin Delano Roosevelt was the 32nd (1933-1945) president of the United States, and the longest serving presdient in the country's history. He got the United States Through the Great Depression, and World War II, and because of this, he is one of the most highly regarded presidents the country has had.

Herbert Hoover

Herbert Clark Hoover was the 31st (1929-1933) president of the United States. Most of the his presidentcy was dominated by the Great Depression, this crisis resulting in his popularity completely tanking. He was relunctant in using government intervention with the issue, which resulted in many seeing his efforts in fixing the Depression as lack luster.

My Findings

First, I compared the most frequent 3 ngram phrases for each president.

I was correct in my assumption of Hoover's usage of The Federal Government. When looking at the Key Word In Context (KWIC) of The Federal Government, he is mostly using it to discuss what he believes it role is towards the American people. Another trend that started to happen during this time was America started to become an isolationist country, where many felt the US needed to keep to itself and prioritize internal affairs over anything else. Hoover's isolationist policies shine through in this KWIC where he does not really talk about any other area except the US. This completely contrasts Roosevelt, which we will see later on.

The United States, he said

As I stated before, Roosevelt's most common phrase is The United States. Because this seems like a pretty typical statement for the country's president to say, I compared Roosevelt's usage to Hoover's.

The United States and Hoover

It appears for Hoover, he mostly discuss The United States in relation to war reparations and federal programs.
