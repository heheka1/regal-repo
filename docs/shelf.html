Digitally Catloging a Library Shelf

For class, I was tasked to picking a shelf in my college's library and recording the books I found into a program called zotero. I then used a website called Palladio to extract data from the books on the shelves.

Along with catalogging the books I found on the library shelf, I went onto the library's website and looked up books that had similar ISBNs that were from other Penn State libraries. I also added some websites that also related to the topics of books I found. The topics that my shelf discussed were gender and sexulality, primarily the discussion of transgender people. I also had a couple of entries that were about prostiution in ancient Greece.

Some Data I Was Able to Get

These graphs are interesting because the highest number of books I cataloged were published between 2005-2010. What makes that data even more fascinating is that there was rapant homophobia and transphobia during the early 2000s, so you wouldn't think a lot of queer books would be published then. That bigotry may be the exact reason why a lot of books were published then, though. It's also interesting that I didn't have any entries that were published between 1980-1995, which would have been at the height of the AIDs crisis. I wounder if that correlates with the lack of these types of books being put in libraries at this time.
